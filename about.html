Small Changes Can Make a World of Difference

Flip It Off is a non-profit organization whose purpose is to spread awareness about the environment and how we can make a positive impact on the world we live in.

Our goal is to see fewer lights kept on at night and to offer alternative solutions to common, everyday environmental issues like the overuse of light energy.

If you are going to leave a room for more than 15 minutes, go ahead and flip that light switch off...or invest in home automation that will do it for you.

They're so much more efficient and they're unaffected by how many times you turn your lights on and off.

Give up those plastic shopping bags for reusable shopping bags (you can get stylish ones and use them over and over).

When dining out, bring along a set of your own fancy utensils...speaking of...

Take your own reusable chopsticks.

While you're at it, just go ahead and take a reusable coffee mug when visiting your favorite barista and reusable straws when sipping your favorite cold beverage at your local food spots.

Ever feel that frustrating feeling when you look around the beach and see trash? Use one of your reusable bags to gather it up and throw it in the nearest waste receptacle.

Probably the most important change for a better world, SMILE at anyone and everyone. A smile goes a long way to the emotional health of those around us. When people feel better, they tend to take better care of things...the Earth is one of those things. So, let's kill each other with kindness and smile at one another a lot more!

The next time you're downtown and need to go from one store to another a couple streets down, walk instead of driving. The exercise is great for your mind and body! You may be surprised to find a new spot you've never noticed before, so go explore.

Last, but definitely not least (and my favorite), save those glass jars some of your groceries come in. They make excellent additions to your kitchenware and have so many uses: from storing a take-along salad to replacing your diminishing supply of drinkware. Not to mention, they already come with everything you need to use them...just peel that label off before sticking them in the dishwasher though...just saying.
